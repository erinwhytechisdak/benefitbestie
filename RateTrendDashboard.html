Nonstop Medical Supplemental Plans
Introduced in 2025 to offset benefit reduction from Gold→Bronze tier transition. 100% employer-paid. Categorized separately from base medical premiums. The $1,000 Backend plan is paired with Kaiser enrollment; the $2,000 Backend plan is paired with Anthem (and Sutter) enrollment.
What is Nonstop? Nonstop Medical Supplemental plans provide "backend exposure" wrap coverage — they reimburse employees for out-of-pocket costs (deductibles, co-insurance) that are higher under bronze-tier plans. The employer pays 100% of these premiums, effectively maintaining the benefit value employees experienced under gold-tier plans. These costs are EMPLOYER PAID and reported separately.

💡 So Why Does the Nonstop Strategy Still Win?
On a pure premium basis, Scenario B costs more than a hypothetical Gold/Platinum renewal for employees under approximately age 44. The real value case rests on three factors not captured in the premium comparison:
  1. Employee out-of-pocket protection: Bronze plans carry deductibles up to $7,000/individual. Without Nonstop, employees absorb that gap entirely. Nonstop eliminates it — effectively giving employees Gold-level coverage at Bronze-tier premiums.
  2. Surplus participation: Employer received $18,000 back in 2025 from underutilized reserves — a structural feature unavailable under a traditional fully-insured Gold/Platinum plan. Actual PMPM utilization came in 39% below the funded level.
  3. Premium trajectory: Gold/Platinum rates have increased ~8.9%/year on average. At Age 45+, Bronze + Nonstop is already cheaper on premiums and that gap widens every year as Gold/Platinum inflation compounds.
