WTW vs. Newfront
2026 Benefits Side-by-Side

A plan-by-plan comparison of medical, Rx, dental, vision, HSA, ancillary, and retirement benefits across two competing offers.

wtw

At a glance

WTW offers three HSA-qualified HDHPs through Anthem BCBS, UnitedHealthcare, or Kaiser (where available), plus rich retirement through a pension + 401(k) non-elective, plus company-paid STD/LTD and 1× salary life.

Newfront offers broader medical plan variety — six plans including traditional PPO (OAP), EPO (OAPIN), HDHP, and Kaiser HMO options — with lower paycheck costs on most plans, a much richer HDHP (employer pays full employee-only premium), and guaranteed HSA funding.

The core tradeoff: WTW is stronger on retirement (pension + 3.5% non-elective 401(k) + ESPP + richer ancillary), while Newfront is stronger on medical plan choice, paycheck cost, and guaranteed HSA funding (richer non-HDHP options, free HDHP for employees, $1K–$3K/yr automatic HSA contributions).

Bottom line If the decision is mostly about medical plan richness, monthly cost, and plan choice, Newfront is the stronger offer — particularly for single employees or those who want non-HDHP options. If the decision is about long-term total rewards, WTW's pension + 401(k) non-elective + ESPP + richer ancillary suite represents meaningfully more annualized value, especially for higher earners with families. The right answer depends on career stage, family tier, and whether retirement accumulation outweighs current paycheck cost.
