3 Forces Pushing Pharmacy Trend Up
Structural drivers — not going away on their own
🧬
Biologics Dominating Spend
Biologics represent nearly 50% of national drug spending despite a tiny fraction of prescriptions. Each new FDA indication multiplies the eligible patient population overnight. 16 new biologics approved in 2024 alone.
📋
Expanded Indications = Demand Multiplier
Semaglutide went from ~$2.6B in 2021 to $54B in 2024. One molecule. Three years. 20x. Each new FDA approval creates a new eligible population — and a new line item on your renewal.
🏭
PBM Opacity and Rebate Walls
Brand manufacturers pay PBMs rebates to maintain formulary position — often making biosimilars harder to adopt even when they exist. Always ask: what's our net-of-rebate spend?
2 Forces Holding It Back
The deflators — real, but insufficient alone
🧪
Biosimilars: Finally Working at Scale
Humira adoption jumped 3% → 28% in 2024. Stelara biosimilars launched Jan 2025 at 80%+ discount. CBO projects expirations + biosimilars save $312B through 2035. But verify your plan is actually capturing the benefit — PBMs don't always pass it through automatically.
🏛️
Medicare Price Negotiation (IRA)
First 10 negotiated prices effective January 2026 (Eliquis, Jardiance, Xarelto, Stelara, Januvia and others). Round 2 — including Ozempic and Wegovy — takes effect 2027. Watch for commercial pricing to shift 12–24 months after each Medicare negotiation closes.

How Employers Are Responding
34% require dietitian or lifestyle program enrollment before coverage. Others add step therapy, BMI thresholds, or restrict to diabetes indication only. Some are narrowing or dropping weight-loss coverage for 2026 after cost shock in 2025.
When Does GLP-1 Relief Come? Honest Answer: Not Soon.
Ozempic's core patent expires ~2026, but Novo Nordisk's extensive secondary patent filings push meaningful generic competition to 2031–2033 at the earliest. Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) is protected to approximately 2036. Compounded semaglutide was a workaround during FDA shortage — FDA ruled shortage over in 2024, banning most compounding. The Most Favored Nation executive order could theoretically compress U.S. prices toward European levels (~$60/month), but implementation timeline and legal viability remain uncertain. Treat GLP-1s as a permanent, growing cost category — not a transitional one.

🔴 Still Burning — No Near-Term Relief
High-spend drugs with full patent protection intact
Keytruda (pembrolizumab) — $29.3B/yr — Cliff: 2028
The world's best-selling drug, used in 40+ cancer types. Core patent 2028, but biologic exclusivity means first biosimilar commercial launch realistically 2028–2029. When it falls, Merck could lose ~$15B in annual revenue within 18 months.
Dupixent (dupilumab) — $15.6B/yr — Cliff: ~2031
Sanofi's atopic dermatitis/asthma blockbuster, expanding into COPD and eosinophilic esophagitis. At ~$28,000/patient/year, one covered employee can materially move a small group's pharmacy trend.
Skyrizi (risankizumab) — $16.1B/yr — Cliff: ~2032+
AbbVie's Humira successor. Aggressively switching Humira patients before biosimilars erode the base. Growing across psoriasis, Crohn's, psoriatic arthritis. No near-term biosimilar threat.
🟢 Relief Is Arriving — Capture the Savings
Expirations creating real employer savings opportunity now
Humira (adalimumab) — $28B peak — Biosimilars HERE
12+ biosimilars on market. Prices down 60–70%. Adoption jumped 3% → 28% in 2024. Action item: verify your PBM is passing Humira biosimilar savings to your plan — not retaining the rebate differential.
Stelara (ustekinumab) — $17B/yr — Biosimilars 2025
First biosimilar launched Jan 1, 2025. Six more preparing at 80%+ discount. Significant savings available for any employer with Crohn's/psoriasis claims.
Eliquis (apixaban) — $26B/yr — Generic: April 2028
Settlement locks generics until April 2028. Negotiated Medicare price effective 2026 will pressure commercial contracts. At $26B system spend, generic entry will be a major deflationary event.
Xarelto (rivaroxaban) — $11B/yr — Generic: 2026
Generic formulations entering market 2026. Alongside Eliquis, anticoagulant category will see significant price compression through 2028. High-AFib/DVT populations should see meaningful savings.

What Smart Employers Are Doing Now
💊
Formulary Tiering & Step Therapy
Requiring less expensive alternatives first can reduce specialty Rx spend 15–25%. Requires clinical criteria that survive Mental Health Parity and state step therapy laws — not every approach is legally viable in every state.
🔁
Active Biosimilar Management
Don't assume your PBM is driving adoption. Ask: what is our biosimilar adoption rate by drug, and what is the rebate pass-through structure for reference products vs. biosimilars? The savings don't always flow automatically.
🏥
Site-of-Care Steering
Infused biologics in hospital outpatient settings cost 200–400% more than an independent clinic. Site-of-care programs can cut costs significantly — but require employee support and network design to be effective.
⚠️
The 1-Employee Scenario
A 50-person group where one employee starts Dupixent (~$28,000/yr) adds 3–4% effective premium increase to the whole group. Add a cancer biologic ($100K+/yr) and pharmacy trend can hit 30%+ before counting the rest. Stop-loss structuring is not optional.
🛡️
Stop-Loss Attachment Strategy
As specialty drugs push toward $500K–$1M+ per patient (gene therapies, CAR-T), $100K–$150K specific attachment points are increasingly inadequate. Aggregate protection design matters as much as the specific layer.
📊
The Data Question to Ask
Can you show me our pharmacy claims broken down by: specialty vs. traditional, top 10 drugs by spend, biosimilar eligibility, and GLP-1 exposure? If your broker can't produce this — that's a signal.
